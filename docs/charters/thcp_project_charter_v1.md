# The Human Capability Project (THCP)

## Project Charter V1 (Draft)

**Status:** Draft for Review
**Project:** The Human Capability Project (THCP)

---

# 1. Purpose

The Human Capability Project exists to increase individual agency through a better understanding of self-reflected capability.

THCP seeks to help individuals recognize, understand, evidence, communicate, and develop the capabilities they acquire throughout life.

---

# 2. Mission

The Human Capability Project exists to help individuals identify, understand, evidence, communicate, and develop the capabilities they acquire through work, projects, responsibilities, learning, hobbies, service, and lived experience.

The project seeks to create frameworks, tools, methodologies, and educational resources that help people better understand what they are capable of and communicate those capabilities accurately and ethically.

---

# 3. Vision

A future in which individuals possess greater agency over how their experiences, skills, capabilities, and identities are understood and represented.

THCP seeks to reduce the gap between what people have actually learned and accomplished and what existing systems are capable of recognizing.

The project is founded on the belief that human capability is broader, richer, and more transferable than traditional credentials, job titles, résumés, or professional classifications often suggest.

---

# 4. Foundational Principles

The following principles form the constitutional foundation of THCP.

## Transparency

THCP shall strive to make its governance, methodologies, assumptions, limitations, and reasoning visible and understandable whenever practical and ethical to do so.

## Honesty

THCP shall represent capabilities, evidence, conclusions, uncertainty, and limitations truthfully and proportionally.

THCP shall not knowingly misrepresent, exaggerate, inflate, or obscure capability.

## Agency

Individuals retain authority over their own participation, representation, and decision-making.

THCP frameworks should increase individual agency rather than replace it.

---

# 5. Governing Principles

The following principles guide the development of THCP frameworks and resources.

## Human Capability Extends Beyond Credentials

Human capability cannot be fully represented by credentials, job titles, certifications, or formal education alone.

## Experience Creates Capability

Capabilities may emerge through employment, projects, responsibilities, self-directed learning, hobbies, caregiving, volunteering, community involvement, and lived experience.

## Evidence Strengthens Understanding

Evidence helps improve understanding, communication, and confidence in capability claims.

Evidence supports understanding but does not replace human judgment.

## Individuals Deserve Accurate Representation

People should have access to frameworks and tools that help them communicate their capabilities accurately and ethically.

---

# 6. Scope

THCP concerns human capability broadly.

Professional capability is an important area of focus but represents only one subset of human capability.

THCP may address capability arising from:

* Employment
* Projects
* Education
* Self-directed learning
* Hobbies
* Volunteering
* Caregiving
* Community leadership
* Personal responsibilities
* Lived experience

THCP includes capability discovery, identification, understanding, evidence, communication, and development within its scope.

---

# 7. Constitutional Structure

THCP is a parent governance, research, and framework initiative.

THCP is not itself a framework.

THCP exists to:

* Establish governance
* Define constitutional principles
* Conduct research
* Publish educational material
* Coordinate framework development
* Govern the THCP ecosystem

Operational capability analysis occurs within frameworks governed by THCP.

---

# 8. Framework Governance

Frameworks operating under THCP governance remain autonomous within their domain while remaining subordinate to THCP governance.

THCP governs the governance of frameworks.

Frameworks govern their own:

* Architectures
* Methodologies
* Workflows
* Models
* Outputs
* Domain-specific rules

provided such governance remains compliant with THCP governance requirements.

Every framework governed by THCP shall possess an approved Framework Charter before a Framework Architecture may be established.

---

# 9. Authority Hierarchy

The constitutional authority hierarchy of THCP is:

1. THCP Governance Rules
2. THCP Charter
3. Framework Charters
4. Framework Architectures
5. Framework Specifications
6. Generated Artifacts

Lower levels may not conflict with higher levels.

---

# 10. Authority, Versioning, and Archival

For any defined scope and artifact class, only one authoritative version may exist at a time.

Superseded authoritative artifacts shall be archived rather than modified or destroyed.

Authority shall be versioned.

Silent replacement of authoritative artifacts is prohibited.

Historical artifacts remain available for transparency, traceability, and institutional memory.

---

# 11. Relationship to PPIP

The Personal & Professional Identity Presentation Framework (PPIP) is the first framework governed by THCP.

PPIP exists within the THCP ecosystem and remains subject to THCP governance.

PPIP serves as an implementation framework focused on helping individuals identify, evidence, organize, and communicate capabilities and experiences in ways that improve understanding, representation, confidence, and agency.

Future frameworks may be established under THCP governance as the initiative evolves.

---

# 12. Charter Authority

This Charter establishes the constitutional purpose, principles, scope, and structure of The Human Capability Project.

Amendments to this Charter shall be issued through versioned successor artifacts.

Only one Charter version may be authoritative at any given time.
